Our Approach to Project Delivery

Totally proposes a structured two-phase approach that ensures clarity, minimises risk, and delivers exactly what Invesis needs.

Why Discovery First? Discovery validates assumptions before significant investment in design and development. Without this phase, estimates remain approximate. With discovery complete, the delivery phase has fixed pricing and clear scope—removing uncertainty.

Collaborative Discovery

Totally's approach begins with understanding before building. Rather than making assumptions about requirements, the discovery phase works closely with the Invesis team to understand current challenges, future ambitions, and how different stakeholders use the website.

This isn't a lengthy requirements-gathering exercise—it's a focused 4-week engagement that maps user needs, validates technical approaches, and creates certainty about what will be built and what it will cost. Discovery removes the guesswork from digital projects.

The outcome is a comprehensive specification that both parties can commit to—detailed enough to provide accurate pricing, flexible enough to accommodate insights discovered during the process.

Parallel Workstreams

Following discovery approval, Totally works across design, content, and development simultaneously. This parallel approach means the visual design evolves while content is being refreshed and technical foundations are being built—reducing overall timeline without compromising quality.

Regular touchpoints ensure the Invesis team sees progress, provides feedback, and maintains control over direction. This isn't a black-box process where everything is revealed at the end.

Built to Empower

Every Totally project focuses on client autonomy. The WordPress platform is configured so the Invesis marketing team can manage content, update pages, and publish news without technical assistance. Training and documentation ensure confidence in using the tools.

This approach stems from 25 years of building platforms that clients actually use and enjoy maintaining—not systems that require ongoing developer support for simple updates.

Technical Approach

Totally proposes optimising the WordPress implementation to remove current limitations whilst maintaining the stability and security required for an institutional-grade platform.

The Invesis marketing team currently manages content within existing constraints. This would involve building a custom WordPress theme with component-based architecture—creating flexible, reusable building blocks that make content management intuitive. Rather than rigid page templates, the team would have modular components they can arrange and configure without technical assistance.

The CMS would be configured to simplify common tasks: publishing news updates, adding project case studies, updating team information, and managing media assets. Clear content workflows and editorial guidelines would ensure consistency whilst empowering the marketing team with operational autonomy.

WordPress Architecture & CMS Optimisation

Multilingual Implementation

As Invesis operates across multiple European markets, the website requires efficient multilingual infrastructure built on reusable components and intelligent content management.

Component-Based Content Architecture

Totally proposes building flexible content blocks and page templates that make multilingual management efficient. Marketing teams can assemble pages from pre-built components (hero sections, feature grids, case study blocks, team profiles) rather than managing monolithic page templates. Each component translates independently—update the "Our Approach" block once, and it updates across all pages where it's used.

This modular approach dramatically reduces translation workload. An intelligent language fallback system ensures visitors always see relevant content: if a page isn't available in Czech, it falls back to English gracefully. The marketing team maintains fewer unique elements whilst delivering comprehensive multilingual experiences.

URL Structure & Regional SEO

Proper technical implementation ensures each language version is discoverable by regional search engines. This includes:

  • Clean URL structure for language versions (`invesis.com/nl/`, `invesis.com/de/`)
  • Hreflang tags telling search engines which language serves which market
  • Regional meta data optimised for local search behaviour
  • Language switching that maintains context (switching from Dutch "About" page goes to German "About" page, not homepage)

The goal is ensuring prospects in Belgium, Germany, or Czech Republic find Invesis through their local Google, Bing, or regional search platforms.

Performance Optimisation

Infrastructure investment websites need to convey credibility and professionalism. Slow load times undermine that perception. Totally proposes implementing comprehensive performance optimisation:

  • Image optimisation with responsive delivery (serving appropriately sized images for each device)
  • Lazy loading for below-the-fold content
  • Caching strategies reducing server load
  • Content delivery network considerations for international audiences
  • Code optimisation ensuring lean, efficient delivery

The target would be achieving consistently fast load times across all markets, regardless of connection quality.

Video Integration & Optimisation

With plans for management board and "who we are" video content, the technical implementation needs to support professional video delivery:

  • Responsive video embedding that works across devices
  • Accessibility considerations including captions and transcripts
  • Performance optimisation with adaptive streaming where appropriate
  • Analytics integration understanding how video content performs
  • Fallback strategies for users on limited bandwidth

Totally recommends evaluating hosting options—self-hosted versus platforms like Vimeo or YouTube—based on privacy requirements, brand control preferences, and budget considerations.

Content & Copywriting / Brand Alignment

Content sits at the heart of the Invesis website—telling stories about infrastructure that serves communities, demonstrating portfolio value, and building stakeholder confidence.

Comprehensive Content Services

Totally proposes working with specialist copywriters from our network who understand B2B infrastructure investment, helping to refresh messaging whilst maintaining the substance and credibility already established.

Content Audit & Strategy

Before writing begins, we would conduct a comprehensive content audit—reviewing what exists, what works, what needs refreshing, and what might be retired. This audit informs the content strategy, identifying priorities and opportunities.

The strategy would address different audience needs: public sector clients need different content than existing clients or potential consortium partners. Clear content pathways would guide each stakeholder group to relevant information efficiently.

Copywriting & Tone of Voice

Working within the Barkley brand guidelines, copywriters would refresh content across all website pages. This involves more than updating facts—it's about transforming tone where appropriate, making complex infrastructure investment concepts accessible without losing credibility.

The tone would remain professional and knowledgeable whilst becoming warmer and more engaging. Infrastructure investment needn't feel dry—Invesis demonstrates genuine impact on communities, and the content should convey that human dimension.

Multi-Jurisdiction Considerations

Content must work effectively across the European markets where Invesis operates. This means:

  • Avoiding jurisdiction-specific assumptions that only apply in one market
  • Using examples and case studies that resonate internationally
  • Structuring content so translations maintain meaning and impact
  • Considering cultural nuances in how infrastructure investment is perceived

Whilst Invesis manages translations in-house, the source content would be written with international audiences in mind from the outset.

Brand Alignment

Working with the Barkley Brand Book

The existing Barkley brand guidelines provide the foundation. Rather than reinventing the visual identity, we propose evolving the digital expression—bringing the brand to life online whilst respecting established guidelines.

This involves understanding the brand positioning, values, and visual language, then translating those into web-appropriate applications. Some brand elements designed for print or presentation may need adapting for digital contexts whilst maintaining the core identity.

Photography Styling & Visual Treatment

Infrastructure photography presents unique challenges. Roads, railways, and hospitals can feel abstract unless shown in use. The Barkley brand book already recognises the importance of humanising these assets—showing people using the facilities that serve their communities.

Totally proposes developing a consistent visual treatment approach that creates cohesive imagery across the website:

  • Image filters and overlays using Invesis brand colours (dark blue, plum) to create visual consistency
  • Framing techniques that draw attention to the human element within infrastructure contexts
  • Composition guidelines helping select or commission photography that aligns with brand values
  • Treatment templates the marketing team can apply to new imagery, reducing reliance on finding "perfect" photos

This approach acknowledges a practical reality: sourcing ideal infrastructure photography with proper permissions is challenging. A consistent visual treatment transforms good photography into branded content that feels cohesive and professional.

Image Styling Process

Rather than requiring perfect source photography, the styling process would work with available imagery:

  • Source photography from Adobe Stock, project consortiums, or Invesis archives
  • Apply brand treatment using filters, overlays, and framing techniques
  • Ensure consistency across the website through systematic application
  • Document guidelines so the marketing team can maintain the approach

The result: a distinctive visual language that differentiates Invesis whilst remaining flexible enough for ongoing content management.

SEO Content Optimisation

Content would be structured for search engine visibility whilst remaining readable and valuable for human audiences:

  • Keyword research understanding how prospects search for infrastructure investment partners
  • On-page optimisation with appropriate headings, meta descriptions, and content structure
  • Regional SEO considerations recognising search behaviour varies across markets
  • Content hierarchy that helps search engines understand site structure and priorities

The goal is ensuring Invesis appears in relevant searches across all operating markets, attracting qualified prospects exploring infrastructure investment partnerships.
